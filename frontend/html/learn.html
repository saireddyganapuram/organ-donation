Laws and Rules Governing Organ Transplantation in India

The primary legislation related to organ donation and transplantation in India is the Transplantation of Human Organs Act, 1994, aimed at regulating removal, storage, and transplantation of human organs for therapeutic purposes and preventing commercial dealings.

Main Provisions of the Act

  1. Brain Death: Brain death identified as a form of death and the certification process defined.
  2. Transplantation Authorization: Allows transplantation from living donors and cadavers (after cardiac or brain death).
  3. Regulatory Bodies:
    • Appropriate Authority (AA): Inspects hospitals, grants registrations, and enforces standards.
    • Advisory Committee: Advises the AA on transplantation matters.
    • Authorization Committee (AC): Reviews cases of living donor transplantation to prevent exploitation and commercial dealings.
    • Medical Board (Brain Death Committee): Certifies brain death with a defined panel of doctors.
  4. Living Donors: Classified as near relatives or non-related donors with specific authorization processes.
  5. Swap Transplantation: Permitted in cases of incompatible living donors.
  6. Organ Donation Authorization: Can be given before or after death, with formal authorization processes outlined.
  7. Organ Retrieval: Permitted from registered hospitals with ICU facilities.
  8. Donor Management Costs: Borne by the recipient, institution, government, or NGOs.
  9. Medico-Legal Cases: Procedure defined to avoid delays and ensure legal compliance.
  10. Transplant Center Registration: Criteria for hospitals to register as transplant centers outlined.
  11. Tissue Banks: Guidelines for tissue storage and retrieval defined.
  12. Transplant Coordinators: Mandatory in all transplant centers.
  13. NGO Registration: NGOs involved in organ transplantation must be registered.
  14. National Networks: Establishment of NOTTO, ROTTO, and SOTTO to facilitate organ donation.
  15. Registry of Donors and Recipients: Central government maintains a registry for transparency.
  16. Penalties: Stringent penalties imposed to prevent illegal organ trade and exploitation.
